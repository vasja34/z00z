<action>
Implement `D3` and `D4` exactly. Specify `validator_mandate_lock_v1` as a
protocol-visible profile built from `RightLeaf::ValidatorMandate` and the live
`validator_mandate` right class, with `validator_mandate_lock_v1` treated as a
proposed Phase 060 profile name instead of an already-live identifier. Bind the
v1 lock grammar to the exact wallet-idea and rights-model fields:
`holder_commitment`, `control_commitment`, `beneficiary_commitment`,
`payload_commitment`, `valid_from`, `valid_until`, `challenge_from`,
`challenge_until`, `use_nonce`, `transition_policy_id`,
`revocation_policy_id`, `disclosure_policy_id`, and `retention_policy_id`.
Mark which fields are mandatory in v1 versus optional or reserved, then bind
locked-asset or locked-amount semantics through `payload_commitment`. Make
ordinary spend of an actively locked asset fail at wallet build-time and
validator acceptance time. Define wallet-visible lock state, unlock or
redelegate transition grammar, reward-claim relation, challenge window, and v1
non-goals. Then add fail-closed coverage for wrong-family proof rejection,
unknown-policy quarantine, right-as-value or voucher-as-cash rejection,
lock-blocks-spend behavior, unlock or redeem replay rejection, approved
post-expiry unlock handling, and unrelated-asset privacy or selectability.
Unlock after expiry is accepted only through the approved unlock transition;
every replay, stale-right, or bypassed-unlock path must fail closed. Do not
widen v1 into slashable bond logic and do not add new primitive leaves.
</action>
<acceptance_criteria>
- `validator_mandate_lock_v1` is specified as a protocol-visible profile.
- the lock profile binds the exact right-field grammar and states which fields
  are mandatory versus optional in v1.
- ordinary spend of a locked asset fails at builder and validator boundaries.
- unlock after expiry is accepted only through the approved unlock transition.
- wallet, validator, watcher, and simulator semantics agree on lock behavior.
- wrong-family proofs, unknown policies, stale rights, replay, and revoked or
  expired rights fail closed.
- unrelated assets remain selectable and hidden from lock-only proofs.
</acceptance_criteria>
