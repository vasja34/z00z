# 056-04 Plan: Journal Lineage, Restart, And Lawful Failover

<objective>

baseline and one lawful same-lineage takeover rule.

Purpose: prevent silent reroute and stale-state acceptance from hiding inside
restart or failover flows.
Output: lineage checks, accept/reject matrix, and restart/import/export proof.
</objective>

<action>
Extend the current journal and recovery seams to preserve route state,
placement, lineage, backend-generation truth, and carry-forward publication
handoff metadata across restart and import/export.
Accept same-lineage standby takeover only when shard id, route generation,
journal lineage, and local root state all match. Reject wrong-lineage,
wrong-generation, stale-root, stale-restart, and split-brain attempts without
silent reroute. Keep RedB local journal as the V1 baseline and explicitly
forbid a shared cross-aggregator WAL as current protocol truth.
</action>

- Illegal failover states reject fail-closed with explicit classes.
- Restart and roundtrip coverage preserve the required lineage metadata.
- Publication handoff metadata survives recovery export/import roundtrip.
- No second durability or failover authority layer appears.
</acceptance_criteria>
