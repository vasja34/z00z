<action>
Extend the runtime-owned transition logic so the publication layer distinguishes
standby join from owner activation, binds ownership change to the committed new
route generation and one activation checkpoint, preserves historical-proof
continuity through transfer, carries forward unchanged shard leaves byte for
byte under mid-window failure, and rejects any silent reroute, pre-activation
publication, wrong-lineage activation, or mixed crash-recovery publication
state. Cover both transfer to a remaining aggregator and transfer to a new
aggregator, and keep topology change config-driven from the checked-in YAML
owner seams.
</action>
<acceptance_criteria>
- Join-as-standby and join-as-owner are distinct protocol states with separate
  evidence and reject paths.
- The mandatory `5x7 -> 6x7` standby-only and owner-activation exemplars stay
  explicit while the transition logic remains topology-generic.
- Route-generation-bound shard transfer preserves old/new route digests and
  historical-proof continuity.
- Transfer to a remaining aggregator and transfer to a new aggregator are both
  evidence-backed and fail closed on wrong generation, wrong checkpoint, or
  wrong lineage.
- Carry-forward preserves unchanged shard-leaf bytes exactly under partial
  failure.
- Crash or restart exposes only the prior visible public root or the exact
  later durable root.
</acceptance_criteria>
