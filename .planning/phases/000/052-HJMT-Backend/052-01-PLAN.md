<action>
Define the deterministic fixed bucket contract used by the real forest backend.
Add `BucketId`, `BucketPolicy`, and `BucketRootLeaf` or equivalent types with
stable byte encoding, bucket-derivation inputs, policy version fields, and
verifier-visible metadata only. Freeze endian and encoding rules in tests, keep
operational counters private unless the design source changes, and keep bucket
ids outside public lookup or mutation APIs. The contract should make future
proof recomputation possible without letting downstream callers treat buckets
as a new semantic hierarchy.
</action>
<acceptance_criteria>
- The bucket policy names `bucket_policy_id`, `bucket_bits`, hash domain,
  canonical encoding, bucket width, minimum bucket count, and compatibility
  generation or equivalent fields.
- `BucketRootLeaf` binds definition, serial, derived bucket id, child asset
  root, and bucket policy identity deterministically.
- Same path and policy derive the same bucket id; different definition, serial,
  asset, or policy inputs change the derived bucket id.
- Invalid policy bounds reject fail-closed, including a single-bucket policy
  whenever the minimum bucket count forbids it.
- Canonical byte encoding is stable and deny-unknown-fields where the chosen
  serialization format can enforce that rule.
- Public asset APIs still accept `AssetPath` only and do not require or accept
  caller-managed bucket ids.
</acceptance_criteria>

  path; reject unknown modes and keep compatibility default.
- Bucket-policy drift could create unverifiable proofs; freeze encoding and
  policy bounds in source-backed tests.
- Public bucket leakage would turn physical layout into semantic authority;
  keep bucket ids and layout names private to storage.
- A temporary skeleton could accidentally become accepted final behavior;
  require later plans and closeout gates to fail if selected in-scope forest
  operations still return unsupported placeholder results.
</threat_model>
