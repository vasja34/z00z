validation, and path-index rebuild needed for a real multi-tree backend.

<action>
Add `ForestCommitJournalEntry`, the journal status lifecycle, and the durable
child-before-parent publication flow. Persist `Prepared` before durable child
commits, record child digests before parent commits, record parent digests
before semantic-root publication, and recover by completing or rolling back to
the last published root only when replayed digests match. Keep in-memory
rollback snapshots as a local guard only; durable recovery authority must come
from the journal. Extend the existing batch preparation substrate in
`tx_plan_batches.rs` when journal-aware batching needs extra metadata instead
of cloning a second batching path under new names.
</action>
<acceptance_criteria>
- The journal has forward-only `Prepared`, `ChildrenCommitted`,
  `ParentsCommitted`, and `RootPublished` states or equivalent durable stages.
- Recovery can handle interruption before child commit, after some child
  commits, after all child commits before parent commit, after parent commit
  before publication, and after publication.
- Child or parent digest mismatch, journal status regression, or replay drift
  reject fail-closed.
- Exported semantic roots are not published before required child-root and
  parent-root evidence is durable.
- Journal-aware batch metadata reuses or extends the existing batching stack
  instead of introducing a parallel commit-preparation lane.
- Repeated recovery does not republish divergent roots after `RootPublished`.
</acceptance_criteria>

<threat_model>
- Partial publication after a crash could leave parents pointing at missing or
  stale children; publish only after durable journal checkpoints.
- Reload drift could turn the path index into a second source of truth; rebuild
  it from committed leaves and validate against forest state.
- Checkpoint metadata drift could silently accept the wrong semantic root; bind
  reload validation to prior and next semantic roots.
</threat_model>
