# Market Positioning

> The strategy sources converge on one public category: Z00Z is strongest as a private rights and settlement layer for wallet-local objects, not as a replacement privacy coin.

## Category Boundary

The competitor report rejects a head-on "Monero killer" or "better Zcash"
frame because deployed privacy-money networks already own maturity, liquidity,
and user expectations in their categories. It also warns against broad private
VM language, where Aztec and Aleo are clearer comparisons.

The preferred Z00Z category is narrower and more defensible: private settlement
for cash-like objects, external asset rights, vouchers, claims, FeeEnvelope
paths, and bounded agent budgets. That category stays close to the live
settlement vocabulary while leaving room for expansion once proof, UX, and
privacy measurements improve.

## Competitive Wedge

The strongest comparison set is not only Monero and Zcash. The competitor
report treats Tari, Beam, Penumbra, and Namada as especially important because
they pressure privacy-plus-assets, confidential assets, and shielded external
asset narratives. Z00Z differentiates only if it keeps private rights over
external anchors, voucher semantics, scoped disclosure, and agent or machine
budgets sharper than a generic shielded-pool or confidential-DeFi story.

## Claim Discipline

The marketing strategy turns that positioning into a control rule: public
language must preserve neutral protocol, self-custody, optional scoped
disclosure, independent issuer responsibility, and checkpoint finality. Copy
should avoid implying official bridges, official exchanges, risk-free
cross-chain privacy, full production OnionNet anonymity, or instant final
settlement before checkpoint verification.

## Launch Sequence

The strategy papers prefer owned knowledge surfaces first, long-form education
and YouTube-style trust media second, selective creator or partner distribution
third, and measurable pilot activation before broad paid amplification. The
image stubs in this source group preserve visual assets for go-to-market
sequencing, competitive boundary, selection discipline, and "privacy alone is
not the category" explanations.

## Proof Obligations

The competitor and marketing notes agree that the architecture is not
self-proving. Stronger public claims need evidence: measured privacy behavior,
wallet safety, checkpoint-finality UX, external route trust tiers, selective
disclosure flows, and bounded agent-spending pilots. Until those exist, the
market story should stay precise rather than maximalist.
