# Expansion Paths

> The corpus does not treat growth as generic “more apps.” It repeatedly extends the same wallet-local settlement logic into concrete ecosystem families.

## Growth Without Changing The Core

The use-case and uniqueness papers argue that private cash, external-asset
rights, policy-shaped money, and agent or machine rights all belong together
because they rely on one shared state model. The agentic and cross-chain papers
push that same model into offline automation and external ecosystem bridges.

## What Stays Stable

Even when the use case changes, the papers keep returning to the same limits:
the public layer should stay narrow, private ownership meaning should stay
local, and service-heavy or custody-heavy layers should remain outside the core.

## Sequencing Constraint

The litepaper and marketing strategy both treat expansion as a staged staircase,
not as a pile of simultaneous claims. Internal settlement hardening comes
first, then richer private asset and claim families, then external asset routes,
then selective disclosure, machine rights, and agent budgets. The go-to-market
and offline-machine image stubs preserve visual provenance for that sequencing;
the agents-rights image stub preserves the visual argument for bounded agent
rights rather than full wallet authority.
